Artemis II

The first crewed Artemis flight brought people back onto the path to the Moon.

Artemis II launched on April 1, 2026 and carried four astronauts on a crewed lunar flyby, making it NASA's first crewed mission around the Moon in more than 50 years.

LaunchApril 1, 2026
Crew4 astronauts
Duration10 days

Crewed systems check

Artemis II validated life support, crew procedures, timing, and deep-space operations under real mission conditions instead of simulation alone.

Artemis II was not just a repeat of Artemis I with people on board. It was the moment the entire program had to prove that deep-space systems, crew procedures, life support, timing, communications, and mission operations could all work together under real conditions.

That made Artemis II a bridge mission. It linked the engineering proof from Artemis I with the more integrated exploration missions NASA wants next. Every successful crew operation, every systems check, and every return milestone helped reduce risk for future lunar campaigns.

In practical terms, Artemis II showed the Artemis architecture was no longer just hardware in development. It had become an operating human spaceflight system.
